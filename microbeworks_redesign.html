The Process

From genome to garment.

01
🧬

Genome Engineering

Using CRISPR-Cas9, we splice chromogenic gene clusters into Bacillus subtilis, switching on dormant pigment-producing pathways with surgical precision.

CRISPR-Cas9
02
🌾

Nutrient Feed

Engineered strains are cultured in bioreactors with precise glucose-mineral media. No petrochemicals. Just carbohydrates and trace minerals.

BIOREACTOR
03
🔬

Fermentation

72-hour aerobic fermentation at 32°C. Microbes multiply exponentially while secreting pigment molecules into the broth at 4-8% dry weight yield.

72HR CYCLE
04
💧

Pigment Extraction

Cell-free broth is centrifuged and membrane-filtered. Pigments are concentrated by nanofiltration — no organic solvents required.

ZERO SOLVENT
05
🎨

Textile Application

Bio-dye formulations drop into existing exhaust dyeing equipment. Compatible with cotton, silk, wool, and synthetic blends. ISO-grade colorfastness.

DROP-IN READY
